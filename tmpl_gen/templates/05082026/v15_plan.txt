v15 architecture plan -- parallel-branching specialists off the frozen v12 baseline
====================================================================================

Vintage label   : v15
Architecture    : independent narrow-SFT branches off v12 (no chaining); optional
                  mergekit combination at the integration step
Wave-1 (W1)     : v12+TAA single-branch experiment (this document)
Author date     : 2026-05-08 (lives in templates/05082026/ alongside v14/v14.1
                  for now; v16 will get its own date-stamped vintage dir)

NOTE: this plan does NOT introduce new IFT shards or templates. v15 W1 reuses
the v14 TAA shard (ift_data_2026_05_08_v14_taa) verbatim. The vintage label
refers to the SFT topology, not the corpus -- we are testing whether the
v14 corpus produces different bench results when applied as an independent
narrow specialist on top of v12, instead of chained as the final pass of a
v14.1-style five-pass topology.


1. Why v15 exists
-----------------

v14.1 shipped at weighted total 49.8 (vs v12's 57.3), a 7.5 pp regression.
The five-pass chain (A -> B -> D-RMS -> D-TAA -> production) demonstrated:

  - Each narrow drill (D-RMS, D-TAA) moved its target axis but regressed
    others
  - Phase A+B broad re-anchor was destructive to base-model general
    capability (CyberSOCEval collapsed below the untrained Qwen base)
  - The production chain (D-TAA on D-RMS) inherited both regressions

The v14.1 post-mortem concluded that **chained sequential SFT causes
catastrophic forgetting between phases** when each phase is broad enough to
overwrite the previous phase's work. v12 worked despite being a 3-phase
chain because each phase was deliberately narrow and small enough to
preserve the prior phase's gains; v14.1 violated that invariant.

v15 is the architectural pivot: instead of chaining narrow specialists
on top of each other, train each one INDEPENDENTLY off the frozen v12
baseline. If multiple specialists are needed in one deployable model, use
mergekit (TIES / DARE-TIES / Linear / Task Arithmetic) at an integration
step rather than chained SFT.

This is the FIRST Glaukopis vintage to use parallel branching. v8, v9,
v11, v12, v13, v14, v14.1 are all sequential chains of varying depth.


2. Wave-1 (W1) scope -- v12+TAA single specialist
--------------------------------------------------

W1 is the cheapest possible test of the v15 hypothesis. Train ONE narrow
specialist (TAA) off v12 and bench. If the standalone branch preserves
v12's other-axis capability while improving TAA, the v15 architecture is
validated and we proceed to W2 (additional specialists; SOC corpus
authoring; mergekit integration). If it regresses other axes, we escalate
to a merge sweep to recover. If TAA itself doesn't move, the bottleneck
is data/recipe (template-bound TAA accuracy ceiling), not topology.

Branch:        v12+TAA  (asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v12-plus-taa)
Base model:    asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v12  (frozen; no retraining)
Dataset:       ift_data_2026_05_08_v14_taa  (32,783 rows; CANON excluded)
Recipe:        v9 narrow-drill (verbatim mirror of v14.1 Phase D-TAA)
                 cutoff 4096, packing ON, lr 5e-6, 1 epoch
                 effective batch 16  (per_device 1 x grad_accum 2 x 8 GPUs)
                 max-samples 33,000
                 eval/save every 100 steps
                 gradient checkpointing OFF (v14.1 hot-fix carried forward)
Launcher:      SFT/autotrain/run_sft_qwen25_14b_v12_plus_taa.sh
Output dir:    SFT/saves/Qwen_Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct/full/v12_plus_taa_${TIMESTAMP}
Wall-time:     ~6-8 h on 8xH100 (matches v14.1 Phase D-TAA observed step-time)


3. Why CANON is excluded from the v12+TAA shard
-------------------------------------------------

ift_data_2026_05_05_v12_taa_canon (10,754 rows) is threat-actor alias
resolution training data only ("APT29 = Cozy Bear = The Dukes = Nobelium
= Midnight Blizzard"). v12 Phase C trained on it at lr 3e-6 and v12's
TAA score reflects that narrow CANON-only training already.

For v12+TAA we want to test whether BROADER TAA training (the full v14
TAA shard, which includes Classic, Hard-Negative, IE, NEG variants and
the full attribution-reasoning template surface) moves the model on TAA
beyond what CANON-only memorisation in v12 produced. Including CANON
again would conflate "did broader TAA help" with "did more CANON drilling
help". The cleaner comparison is broad-shard-only.


4. W1 decision criteria (post-bench)
-------------------------------------

After the v12+TAA bench result lands, classify the outcome and act:

  Outcome A -- "ship as standalone specialist":
    TAA Classic combined > v12 baseline + 5pp
    AND every other measured axis >= v12 - 2pp (within bench noise)
    AND weighted total >= v12 (currently 57.3)
    -> v15 architecture validated; v12+TAA deploys as a TAA specialist
    -> proceed to W2: additional specialists (RMS expansion, SOC corpus
       authoring); mergekit integration only needed when N >= 2 specialists
       must combine into one deployable model

  Outcome B -- "merge to recover":
    TAA Classic combined > v12 baseline + 5pp
    BUT one or more axes regressed > 2pp vs v12
    -> narrow SFT from healthy v12 still caused some forgetting;
       Linear/Task-Arithmetic merge with v12 should recover the lost axes
       at the cost of some TAA gain
    -> author SFT/utils/run_merge.sh + mergekit conda env;
       run merge sweep over alpha in {0.5, 0.7, 0.85, 0.95} (alpha = v12
       weight; (1-alpha) = v12+TAA branch weight)
    -> bench all candidates; pick best weighted-total winner

  Outcome C -- "data/recipe issue":
    TAA Classic combined <= v12 baseline
    -> bottleneck is template-bound TAA accuracy ceiling (5% accurate vs
       93% plausible pattern observed in v14.1) rather than topology
    -> halt v15 W1; re-examine TAA shard for hard-negative coverage;
       consider authoring v15-taa-v2 shard with explicit
       (correct + 2-3 close-but-wrong) attribution pairs

  Outcome D -- "net regression regardless of TAA":
    Weighted total < v12 (57.3)
    -> diagnose before any merge; do not deploy


5. v15 architecture going forward (W2+ -- contingent on W1 outcome)
--------------------------------------------------------------------

If W1 lands as Outcome A or B, the v15 architecture as a whole is:

      Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct
            |
            v
          v12  (frozen baseline, already shipped)
          /|\
         / | \
        v  v  v
      +TAA +RMS +SOC ...   (independent narrow branches off v12)
        \ | /
         \|/
          v
       merged v15  (mergekit; TIES or Linear depending on W1 result)
                   OR multi-specialist routed deployment if merge fails

The W2+ branches not yet authored:
  - v12+SOC: requires authoring CyberSOCEval-shaped training corpus
    (long sandbox JSON -> behavioral classification; long TI report
    -> structured Q&A). ~30-50K rows for malware track + ~20-40K for TI
    track. ~3-5 weeks of corpus work. Highest-ROI specialist (v12 has
    zero SOC training).
  - v12+RMS-v2: existing v14_rms shard (12,608 rows) trained off v12
    instead of v14p1-ab. Cheap (~6 h) and tests the v15 pattern on a
    second axis. Low priority because v12 already includes Phase B
    RMS training; expected gains are small.
  - v12+TAA-v2: re-author TAA shard with explicit hard-negative
    attribution pairs to break the 5%-accurate ceiling. Templates
    work (~2-3 weeks) before training (~6-8 h). Medium priority;
    contingent on W1 outcome A or B (if outcome C, this becomes high
    priority).

Wave-2 sequencing decisions deferred until W1 result lands.


6. v16 forward note
--------------------

When v16 is designed it will get its own self-contained vintage directory
(tmpl_gen/templates/<v16_date>/) with a fresh corpus, fresh templates,
and fresh plan from scratch. v15's templates dir (this one, 05082026/)
will remain frozen as the v14/v14.1/v15 vintage record. Per the project
convention each vintage's templates dir is a self-contained snapshot of
the corpus + plan + bench targets that produced its checkpoints.
