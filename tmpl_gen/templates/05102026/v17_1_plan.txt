1. Why v17.1 exists (one-paragraph overview)
---------------------------------------------

1.1 v17 was built on top of asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v16 with the
    Sophia-CTI-Templates-v17.txt manifest. The post-bench result was
    Outcome D per v17_plan.txt §4: the chained SFT regressed every measured
    Athena axis simultaneously and did NOT lift CSE-Malware or CSE-TI.

1.2 Forensic analysis of SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_11_v17_cse.json (16,548
    train rows) revealed the cause is a corpus defect, not a chaining
    failure. The v17 manifest hard-coded the answer letter list into the
    Answer block of every JS.CSE.* template (e.g. `"correct_answers":
    ["A", "B"]`) and declared no Shuffle directive. The engine therefore
    rendered every triple of a given template with the SAME literal letter
    list, producing only 4 unique correct_answers tuples across the entire
    corpus:
        ["A","B"]      = 50.7%   (5 templates)
        ["C"]          = 24.3%   (4 templates)
        ["A","B","D"]  = 18.5%   (3 templates)
        []             =  6.6%   (2 templates)
    Letters E/F/G/H NEVER appeared as correct answers anywhere in the
    corpus. From the optimizer's perspective the input distribution was
    essentially noise relative to the output: the model can satisfy the
    training loss almost entirely by learning "emit A,B regardless of
    input". This is structural mode-collapse and is the most plausible
    single cause of the v17 -> v16 regression on every axis.

1.3 v17.1 isolates this single variable. The manifest body is byte-
    identical to v17 except that every template now declares
    `Shuffle: mcq_multi`, a new engine directive that permutes the option
    block (A-H), parses the JSON-letter-set answer marker (wrapped
    <json_object>{...}</json_object> or bare {...}), and rewrites the
    correct_answers list to the shuffled positions. Single-shape
    `Shuffle: mcq` and all other render paths are untouched.

1.3a Second root cause discovered during the v17.1 build dry-run
    (2026-05-10): tmpl_gen/scripts/tmpl_docx2json.py truncated every
    JS.CSE.* template body at the blank line that follows the <desc>
    wrapper. Its question-collection loop appended only consecutive
    "[A-Z])" option lines after the initial Question: header and broke
    out on any other line shape, so the rendered triples for v17 (and
    the first v17.1 dry-run) contained Instruction + a Question: body
    truncated at </desc> + Answer: <hard-coded letter list>, with the
    "Based on the intelligence above ..." prose paragraph and ALL the
    A) ... E) option lines silently dropped. This explains why even a
    correctly-firing mcq_multi shuffler produced zero option-line matches
    in its regex: the option lines were never in the rendered text.
    The fix in tmpl_docx2json.py mirrors the answer-collection semantics:
    after the Question: header, continue collecting subsequent lines
    (preserving internal blank lines, stripping trailing) until Answer:,
    a {force ...} constraint, a Sample/Shuffle/Count/... sentinel
    prefix, or the next template ID line. v12/v16 templates are unaffected
    because their Question: bodies are single-line; the change only
    extends what the parser will accept.

1.4 The hypothesis under test: with a properly-randomized correct_answers
    distribution, the chained-SFT recipe IS sufficient to lift the CSE
    axes without regressing the v16 TAA-attribution head. If v17.1
    reproduces the v17 regression, the problem is architectural (chained
    SFT competes with the v16 head) and a v18 mergekit alpha sweep is
    indicated. If v17.1 lifts CSE-Malware and CSE-TI without TAA loss,
    the problem was the corpus and chained SFT is validated.


2. Pipeline (v17 watcher with v17.1 paths and one new gate)
------------------------------------------------------------

Build orchestrator: _v17_1_build/watcher.sh (forked from _v17_build/watcher.sh).
The pipeline phases mirror v17 verbatim with one addition (Phase 6c) and
one path change (the row-count gate plan):

  Phase 1   poll make_dataset.sh PID         -- carry from v17
  Phase 2   validate raw json                -- carry from v17
  Phase 4   TAA actor-balance                -- carry (per-actor cap 60)
  Phase 5   dedup against eval sets          -- carry (n=13, drop>=50)
  Phase 6   row-count gate                   -- v17.1 thresholds (same axes
                                                as v17, target band carried)
  Phase 6b  licence-allowlist gate           -- carry from v17
  Phase 6c  letter-balance gate              -- NEW: rejects the build if
                                                any of A-E carries <8% or
                                                >32% of correct slots, OR
                                                any single combo carries
                                                >=15% of rows, OR <20
                                                distinct combos appear.
                                                The 20-combo floor reflects
                                                the manifest's combinatorial
                                                ceiling of 26 (= 1 zero-correct
                                                + C(5,1) + C(5,2) + C(5,3));
                                                an earlier draft's 50-combo
                                                threshold was unsatisfiable
                                                given the manifest's letter
                                                cardinality.
  Phase 7   stratified shuffle               -- carry
  Phase 8   val/train split                  -- carry (v17.1 paths via
                                                _v17_1_build/build_val_slice.py)

Generation step (Sophia pipeline) is invoked manually before the watcher:

  bash tmpl_gen/data_generation/make_dataset.sh \
       tmpl_gen/templates/05102026/Sophia-CTI-Templates-v17.1.txt \
       _v17_1_build/triples \
       SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1.raw.json \
       10 3500
  echo "PID=$!" > _v17_1_build/build.pid
  nohup bash _v17_1_build/watcher.sh > _v17_1_build/watcher.log 2>&1 &

Output paths (data path datestamp = 2026_05_12):

  Raw         : SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1.raw.json
  Balanced    : SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1.balanced.json
  Clean       : SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1.json
  Shuffled    : SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1.shuffled.json
  Validation  : SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1_val.json   (~50 rows / axis)
  Train (CSE) : SFT/data/ift_data_2026_05_12_v17_1_cse.json   (sole training shard)

4. Decision criteria (post-bench)
----------------------------------

Inherits v17_plan.txt §4 outcome classification verbatim. Additional
v17.1-specific interpretation overlay:

  Outcome A or B (CSE up vs v16):
    -> the v17 regression was the corpus bug, not the chained-SFT mechanism.
       v17.1 ships as the chained CSE specialist (Outcome A) or proceeds
       to the mergekit alpha sweep (Outcome B). v17 (mode-collapsed) is
       deprecated on HF.

  Outcome C or D (CSE flat or net regression):
    -> the v17 regression was at least partly architectural; corpus fix
       is necessary but not sufficient. Author v18 with one of:
         (i) mergekit alpha sweep against v16 weights, or
         (ii) parallel-branch architecture (v15-style) with v17.1 corpus,
              freezing the v16 TAA head via DPO-style replay loss.
