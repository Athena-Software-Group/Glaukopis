1. Why v18.2.2 exists -- the v18.2 / v18.2.1 gate-package sub-floor
-------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 The v18.1 chain delivered the designed CSE uplift (CSE-TI +16 pp,
    CSE-Malware +16 pp) but eroded RMS combined_f1 by 11.6 pp at the
    CSE stage (v18.1-core 57.69 -> v18.1-cse 46.34). v18.2 was a
    Stage 4 touch-up over v18.1-cse using a 3-shard interleave at probs
    0.25/0.40/0.35 (Phase A / Phase B / TAA) with --max-samples 2400 per
    dataset (~1500 optimizer steps, ~80-100 min on 4xH100). v18.2
    recovered RMS to 54.72 (within 0.28 pp of the 55.0 gate) and held
    8 of 10 axes; MCQ stuck at 62.33 (gate 70.0).

1.2 v18.2.1 was a prob-and-step rebalance designed to recover MCQ
    (Phase A 0.25 -> 0.35) and close the RMS hairline (Phase B 0.40 ->
    0.45) while dropping TAA share (0.35 -> 0.20) on the hypothesis
    that the standalone TAA shard's short-form structured generation
    competed with MCQ's letter-decoder pattern. Step count was bumped
    to --max-samples 3000 per dataset (~1667 steps, ~95-115 min). It
    backfired:

      axis              v18.1-cse  v18.2    v18.2.1   gate    delta
      ----              ---------  -----    -------   ----    -----
      MCQ                  72.03   62.33     63.17    70.0    +0.84 (within noise)
      RMS combined_f1      46.34   54.72     50.37    55.0    -4.35 (INVERTED)
      ATE                  58.60   63.20     62.40    63.0    -0.80 (sub-floor)
      RCM                  69.60   72.55     66.80    67.5    -5.75 (sub-floor)
      VSP                  76.29   83.87     82.65    80.0    -1.22 (PASS)
      TAA Classic comb       --    47.50     47.00    40.0    -0.50 (PASS)
      CSE-TI               36.07   41.25     41.79    34.0    +0.54 (PASS)
      CSE-Malware          22.37   24.14     23.48    20.0    -0.66 (PASS)
      CyberMetric-2K       87.70   88.95     89.35    85.5    +0.40 (PASS)
      CyberMetric-10K      83.40   83.94     84.17    81.0    +0.23 (PASS)

    v18.2.1 fails 4 gates vs v18.2's 2 -- a strict regression of the
    gate package.

1.3 Trade-ratio diagnosis (full derivation in 05132026/v18_2_plan.txt
    §8.2.3): v18.2's MCQ-for-RMS exchange |dRMS/dMCQ| = 0.86 vs
    v18.2.1's 0.45 -- v18.2.1 traded MCQ damage at half the RMS-recovery
    rate. First-order implication: the v18.2 prob mix was the right
    one, and v18.2.1 pushed past the optimum on BOTH probs and step
    count. Mechanism for the Phase B prob-bump backfire: Phase B is a
    4-task shard (rms+ate+vsp+rcm), so raising its interleave probability
    raises ALL four signals in lockstep -- the RMS-specific ratio
    inside the mix did not improve; the +22% step bump then over-
    trained the dilution. Confirmation: ATE -0.80 and RCM -5.75 also
    regressed under the larger Phase B share, consistent with Phase B's
    multi-task structure.

1.4 v18.2.2 is therefore RECIPE-DRIVEN, not corpus-driven. No template
    or manifest changes. The Stage 1, 2, 3, AND v18.2 dataset shards
    are untouched. v18.2.2 adds exactly one new training launcher and
    one new bench wrapper; the only knob differences vs v18.2 are
    --max-samples (2400 -> 1500) and a new push target (the v18-2-2 HF
    repo).

3. Recipe (Stage 4 only)
------------------------

3.1 Hardware: 4xH100 80GB. Single phase:
      Stage 4 (multi-shard replay; cutoff 16384, packing off, lr 1e-6,
              effective batch 4, --max-samples 1500 PER DATASET,
              1 epoch, save every 200 steps, intra-training eval
              DISABLED, mix_strategy=interleave_under, interleave_probs
              0.25 / 0.40 / 0.35 over Phase A / Phase B / TAA)

3.2 Sample-count math: --max-samples is applied PER dataset in
    LlamaFactory (each shard subsampled BEFORE interleaving). With
    interleave_under + probs P, the resulting interleaved dataset has
    size N / max(P), where N is the per-dataset cap and max(P) is the
    highest interleave weight (Phase B at 0.40). So 1500 / 0.40 = 3750
    final training samples, ~937 optimizer steps at eff_bs 4.

3.3 Datasets (interleaved): see §2 UNCHANGED block. JSON shard
    inventory + sizes in READY.
      Eval : NONE (intra-training eval disabled; same rationale as
             v18.2 / v18.2.1; LlamaFactory loader keys datasets by
             name and the 3:3 shard-vs-prob alignment requirement
             cannot be satisfied without a fresh data build for a
             touch-up where sign-off is via the bench suites). Train
             loss is logged at logging_steps=5 throughout.

3.4 Push target: asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v18-2-2 (NEW repo;
    v18-2 and v18-2-1 retained for regression comparison). Promotion
    to headline ship is by alias swap, NOT by overwrite of
    v18.1-cse / v18-2 / v18-2-1.

3.5 Hypothesis (full version in 05132026 §8.3): the v18.2 prob mix is
    correct and the v18.2.1 regression is over-exposure damage from too
    many Stage 4 steps -- the catalog shard erodes Phase A and CSE drill
    circuits when the run is too long. Reducing the step count without
    changing probs should preserve the RMS gain while reducing MCQ
    damage and protect ATE/RCM from sliding below their floors.

3.6 Why a low-LR replay (and not a deeper Phase B re-install): see
    05132026 §4. At 1e-6 the gradient magnitude is small enough to
    nudge Phase-B-installed circuits back toward their post-Phase-B
    optimum (which CSE displaced with off-distribution updates) without
    disturbing the CSE drill's short-form pattern.


4. Sign-off and falsification (refines 05132026 §5 and §7.4 for v18.2.2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 v18.2.2 MUST PASS:
      RMS combined_f1 >= 55.0   (v18.2: 54.72; expected: 53-56 given
                                  -50% steps but same prob mix --
                                  monotone-with-steps assumption gives
                                  a slight RMS loss vs v18.2; if RMS
                                  lands in 53-55 zone the trade-ratio
                                  hypothesis is partly confirmed and
                                  the Phase A anchor escalation in §5(a)
                                  is the next test.)
      ATE             >= 63.0   (v18.2: 63.20; expected: stable.)
      VSP             >= 80.0   (v18.2: 83.87; expected: stable.)

4.2 v18.2.2 MUST NOT REGRESS:
      MCQ accuracy    >= 70.0   (v18.2: 62.33; expected: 65-70. If MCQ
                                  lands 67-70 the trade-ratio hypothesis
                                  is confirmed and the anchor escalation
                                  closes the final gap.)
      CSE-TI accuracy >= 34.0   (v18.2: 41.25; expected: stable or up
                                  -- less Stage 4 = less CSE drift.)
      CSE-Malware acc >= 20.0   (v18.2: 24.14; expected: stable or up.)
      TAA Classic comb >= 40.0  (v18.2: 47.50; expected: 45-50; risk is
                                  undershooting rather than over-shooting
                                  given same TAA prob and -50% steps.)
      RCM accuracy    >= 67.5   (v18.2: 72.55; expected: 70-73.)
      CyberMetric-2K  >= 85.5   (v18.2: 88.95; expected: stable.)
      CyberMetric-10K >= 81.0   (v18.2: 83.94; expected: stable.)

4.3 v18.2.2 is NOT expected to reach v17.1 RMS parity (62.64 strict f1).
    Closing the v17.1 RMS gap requires v18.3 (a Core-stage Phase B
    re-tune at the source); see 05132026 §5.5(c).

4.4 If 4.1 passes and 4.2 holds, asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v18-2-2
    is promoted to the headline v18.2 ship and the master results table
    is updated. v18-2 and v18-2-1 repos are retained for regression
    comparison but unaliased from production.


5. v18.2.2 escalation paths (mirrors 05132026 §8.5)
---------------------------------------------------

  (a) MCQ recovers (>=70) but RMS misses (53-55): "late Phase A anchor"
      pass -- chain a small Phase-A-only Stage 4b on top of v18.2.2
      (--max-samples 500, lr 1e-6, eff_bs 4, ~10-15 min). Goal: bias
      the final gradient updates toward MCQ-decoder circuits without
      disturbing the Phase B install. Push as v18-2-2-anchor.

  (b) MCQ misses (<=68) AND RMS passes (>=55): the step cut was too
      aggressive on MCQ recovery. Try v18.2.3 with --max-samples 2000
      (between v18.2.1's 3000 and v18.2.2's 1500).

  (c) Both MCQ and RMS still miss but trade ratio is BETTER than v18.2
      (|dRMS/dMCQ| > 0.86): the smaller-Stage-4 hypothesis is correct
      but needs a different prob skew. Try probs 0.30/0.35/0.35 with
      --max-samples 1500.

  (d) Both MCQ and RMS still miss and trade ratio is WORSE than v18.2:
      Stage 4 multi-shard has hit its ceiling. Escalate to v18.3
      (Core-stage Phase B re-tune at the source per 05132026 §5.5(c)).
      v18.2 remains the closest variant and ships with a documented
      gate exception, or is dropped in favour of v18.1-cse + the
      cse-rms single-shard touch-up.
