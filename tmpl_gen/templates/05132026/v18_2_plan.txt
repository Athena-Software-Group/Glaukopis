1. Why v18p2 exists -- the v18.1 CSE-stage RMS regression
---------------------------------------------------------

1.1 The v18.1 chain delivered the designed CSE uplift (CSE-TI 19.9 ->
    36.07, +16.2 pp; CSE-Malware 6.6 -> 22.37, +15.8 pp) and held MCQ
    and RCM (post-chain MCQ 72.03, RCM 69.60). The chain is healthy on
    its primary objective. However the post-CSE benchmark on 2026-05-13
    isolated a singular regression on RMS:

        axis    Core     +TAA     +CSE     dCore->CSE   dTAA->CSE
        ----    -----    -----    -----    ----------   ---------
        RMS f1c 57.69    57.91    46.34    -11.35       -11.57
        ATE     65.60    63.20    58.60     -7.00        -4.60
        VSP     84.30    83.16    76.29     -8.01        -6.87
        RCM     71.30    61.10    69.60     -1.70        +8.50
        MCQ     67.37    69.03    72.03     +4.66        +3.00

    (RMS combined_f1 = (strict_f1 + plausible_f1) / 2; see
     SFT/test/pipelines/evaluation/athena_cti_eval.py:430.)

1.2 Diagnosis (from per-stage benchmark response files at
    SFT/test/responses/asg-ai_athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v18-1-{core,
    taa,cse}/ on the bench host, 2026-05-13):

      RMS damage is entirely Stage-3 (CSE). The TAA stage leaves RMS
      untouched (+0.22 pp, noise-equivalent). CSE strips 11.6 pp of
      combined F1 in one stage. ATE and VSP degrade gradually across
      both stages with CSE costing more than TAA. RCM has the inverse
      pattern: TAA harms it (-10.2), CSE accidentally repairs it
      (+8.5) -- likely due to multi-letter MCQ rows in the CSE shard
      whose shape overlaps with RCM's eval shape.

      Root cause is structural to the v18.1 chain design: the Phase B
      shard (ift_data_2026_05_11_v18p1_core_b_rms_ate_vsp_rcm) is the
      ONLY place RMS, ATE, VSP, and RCM are ever installed. The +TAA
      and +CSE stages train on disjoint shards (ift_..._v18p1_taa,
      ift_..._v18p1_cse) that do not touch these axes. RMS is the
      most fragile of the four because (i) it is generative and
      scored on lexical overlap (small style drift -> big F1 hit),
      and (ii) the CSE drill geometry (cutoff 4096, packing on,
      short structured outputs) is the geometric opposite of Phase
      B's RMS install (cutoff 16384, packing off, long-form free
      attention).

1.3 v18p2 is therefore RECIPE-DRIVEN, not corpus-driven. No template
    or manifest changes. The Stage 1, 2, 3 launchers, datasets, and
    row-count gates are untouched. v18p2 adds exactly one new artifact:
    a Stage 4 launcher that interleaves three existing shards (Phase A
    MCQ-bearing + Phase B catalog + standalone TAA Classic) at 1/5th
    the Phase B LR over the published v18.1-cse checkpoint.

1.4 v18p2 EXPERIMENT (cse-rms; superseded). The first attempt at this
    Stage 4 (run_sft_qwen25_14b_v18p1_rms_replay.sh, pushed to
    asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v18-1-cse-rms) replayed the Phase
    B shard alone. It recovered RMS combined_f1 +11.3 pp and ATE
    +5.8 pp -- but introduced new regressions of MCQ -4.0 pp and TAA
    Classic -12.0 pp because those data types were absent from the
    single-shard mix. v18.2 replaces that recipe with a three-shard
    interleave that protects MCQ and TAA Classic alongside the catalog
    drill. The cse-rms repo is retained for posterity / regression
    comparison; the multi-shard build pushes to a NEW repo (v18-2).

3. Touch-up recipe (Stage 4 only)
---------------------------------

3.1 Hardware: 4x H100 80GB. Single phase:
      Stage 4 (multi-shard replay; cutoff 16384, packing off, lr 1e-6,
              effective batch 4, --max-samples 2400 PER DATASET,
              1 epoch, save every 200 steps, intra-training eval
              DISABLED (see 3.2), mix_strategy=interleave_under,
              interleave_probs 0.25 / 0.40 / 0.35 over Phase A /
              Phase B / TAA)

      Sample-count math: --max-samples is applied per dataset in
      LlamaFactory (each shard subsampled BEFORE interleaving).
      With interleave_under + probs P, the resulting interleaved
      dataset has size N / max(P), where N is the per-dataset cap
      and max(P) is the highest interleave weight (Phase B at 0.40).
      So 2400 / 0.40 = 6000 final training samples, ~1500 optimizer
      steps at eff_bs 4.

3.2 Datasets (interleaved):
      Stage 4 : ift_data_2026_05_11_v18p1_core_a_kb_mcq_taa_soc_cm_ms_yn
                (Phase A; MCQ + KB + TAA + SOC + CM + MS + YN coverage;
                 protects the MCQ axis that cse-rms eroded)         prob 0.25
                ift_data_2026_05_11_v18p1_core_b_rms_ate_vsp_rcm
                (Phase B catalog drill; the only place RMS/ATE/VSP/RCM
                 are ever installed; primary recovery target)       prob 0.40
                ift_data_2026_05_11_v18p1_taa
                (standalone TAA Classic shard, identical to the
                 Stage 2 +TAA shard; protects TAA Classic axis that
                 cse-rms eroded by -12.0 pp)                        prob 0.35
      Eval    : NONE -- intra-training eval is DISABLED for this run.
                LlamaFactory's data_args validator requires
                len(eval_dataset) == len(interleave_probs), and its
                loader keys datasets by name -- so listing core_val
                twice (the natural eval for Phase A and Phase B,
                which share the unified core_val) silently dedupes
                to 2 unique entries against 3 probs and crashes
                interleave_datasets with a numpy probability/source
                size mismatch. Producing three truly distinct eval
                shards (phase_a_val / phase_b_val carved out of
                core_val) would require a data build; not worth it
                for a touch-up where sign-off is via the AthenaBench/
                CSE/CM bench suites and intra-training eval loss is
                monitoring-only. Train loss is still logged at
                logging_steps=5 throughout the run.

3.3 Recipe deltas vs cse-rms (the single-shard predecessor):
      Datasets        1 (Phase B)      -> 3 (interleaved)
      Mix strategy    none             -> interleave_under (probs 0.25/0.40/0.35)
      --max-samples   5000             -> 2400/dataset (~6000 interleaved total)
      Intra-train eval ON              -> OFF (see 3.2)
      LR              1e-6                1e-6  (UNCHANGED)
      cutoff          16384               16384 (UNCHANGED)
      packing         off                 off   (UNCHANGED)
      Effective batch 4                   4     (UNCHANGED)
      save steps      200                 200   (UNCHANGED)

    Recipe deltas vs Phase B (the install run for the catalog shard):
      LR              5e-6     -> 1e-6     (1/5th -- replay, not install)
      Effective batch 8        -> 4        (half -- gentler updates)
      --max-samples   70000    -> 2400/ds  (~6000 interleaved total; ~12x)
      Datasets        1                3   (multi-axis protection)
      cutoff          16384       16384    (UNCHANGED -- preserve geometry)
      packing         off         off      (UNCHANGED -- preserve geometry)
      eval/save steps 400      -> 200      (denser checkpointing; eval off)
      gradient ckpt   on          on       (UNCHANGED; LF default)
      DS offload      off (8x)  -> on (4x) (memory-pressure adjustment)

3.4 Push target: asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v18-2
3.5 Wallclock (estimate): ~80-100 min on 4xH100 (~1500 optimizer
    steps at ~3-4 s/step at this cutoff with offload on; eval
    disabled removes ~1-2 min per checkpoint round).

3.6 Mix-probability rationale. The 0.25 / 0.40 / 0.35 split is
    weighted toward the largest observed regression deltas:
      - Phase B (0.40): RMS install is the only catalog drill in
        the chain and the most fragile; the cse-rms run showed
        these probabilities recover +11 pp at this exposure.
      - TAA (0.35): cse-rms regressed TAA Classic by -12 pp (the
        largest delta), so TAA gets the second-largest share to
        re-cement the Stage 2 specialization.
      - Phase A (0.25): MCQ regressed only -4 pp in cse-rms; Phase
        A also dilutes with non-MCQ rows (KB / SOC / CM / MS / YN)
        so a smaller share is sufficient. Phase A's TAA rows are
        a no-op overlap with the standalone TAA shard at worst.


4. Why a low-LR replay (and not a deeper Phase B re-install)
------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 At Phase B's LR (5e-6) a Stage 4 over the v18.1-cse base would
    re-install RMS but at the cost of the CSE letter-pattern circuits
    in the upper layers -- effectively rewinding to a post-Phase-B
    state with extra steps, discarding the +15-16 pp CSE gain that
    v18.1 was designed to deliver.

4.2 At 1e-6 the gradient magnitude is small enough to nudge Phase-B-
    installed circuits back toward their post-Phase-B optimum (which
    CSE displaced with off-distribution updates) without disturbing
    the CSE drill's short-form pattern.

4.3 The Phase B shard contains ATE and VSP rows in addition to RMS,
    so the catalog-share replay incidentally replays those
    installations. RCM is also in the shard but recovers naturally
    during CSE; RCM rows in the touch-up are a no-op at worst.

4.4 The Phase A and TAA shards exist in the mix purely as anti-
    forgetting buffers, not as primary install vehicles. Their job
    is to keep MCQ and TAA Classic distributions in the gradient
    signal so the catalog-share's updates do not drag those axes
    off-distribution (the failure mode that the cse-rms experiment
    exposed).


5. Sign-off and falsification
-----------------------------

5.1 v18p2 (Stage 4 standalone benchmark) MUST PASS:
      RMS combined_f1 >= 55.0   (sanity: must beat v18.1-cse's 46.34
                                  and approach v18.1-taa's 57.91;
                                  cse-rms achieved 57.6, set the floor)
      ATE             >= 63.0   (must restore most of the 7-pp Stage-3
                                  loss; v18.1-taa was 63.20)
      VSP             >= 80.0   (must restore most of the 8-pp Stage-3
                                  loss; v18.1-core was 84.30)

5.2 v18p2 MUST NOT regress (>=2 pp drop) on:
      CSE-TI accuracy >= 34.0   (v18.1-cse: 36.07 -- protect CSE gain)
      CSE-Malware acc >= 20.0   (v18.1-cse: 22.37 -- protect CSE gain)
      MCQ accuracy    >= 70.0   (v18.1-cse: 72.03 -- cse-rms regressed
                                  to ~68; multi-shard MUST recover)
      TAA Classic     >= 40.0   (cse-rms regressed by -12 pp; multi-
                                  shard MUST hold within 2 pp of v18.1-
                                  cse's TAA Classic baseline)
      RCM accuracy    >= 67.5   (v18.1-cse: 69.60)
      CyberMetric-2K  >= 85.5   (v18.1-cse: 87.70)
      CyberMetric-10K >= 81.0   (v18.1-cse: 83.40)

5.3 v18p2 is NOT expected to reach v17.1 RMS parity (62.64 strict f1).
    Phase B itself installed RMS at 56.34 strict, ~6 pp under v17.1 --
    so even a perfect replay caps near 56-58 strict. Closing the v17.1
    RMS gap requires v18.3 (a Core-stage Phase B re-tune; see 5.5c).

5.4 If 5.1 passes and 5.2 holds, asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-
    v18-2 is promoted to the headline v18.2 ship and the master
    results table is updated. The cse-rms repo is retained for
    posterity but unaliased from production.

5.5 If 5.1 fails on RMS only, options in escalation order:
      (a) Re-run with --max-samples 10000 (deeper replay) and probs
          tilted toward Phase B (e.g. 0.20 / 0.50 / 0.30).
      (b) Re-run with --lr 5e-7 (gentler) AND --max-samples 10000.
      (c) Build v18.3 with a re-tuned Phase B (deeper RMS install
          at the source) and re-chain TAA + CSE downstream.

5.6 If 5.2 fails on TAA Classic or MCQ, the corresponding shard's
    interleave probability was too low. Re-run with the failing
    axis bumped (e.g. raise TAA prob from 0.35 to 0.45 and drop
    Phase B to 0.30; or raise Phase A prob from 0.25 to 0.35 and
    drop Phase B to 0.30). Keep --max-samples constant so the
    touch-up does not lengthen disproportionately.

5.7 If 5.2 fails on CSE (CSE-TI/CSE-Malware regress), the touch-up
    was too aggressive overall. Lower --max-samples (4000) and/or
    LR (5e-7) and re-run.

7.2 Diagnosis of the two failures.

      MCQ. The multi-shard recipe was designed specifically to protect
      MCQ via Phase A's MCQ-bearing rows, but MCQ regressed MORE than
      in cse-rms (which had no Phase A at all): -9.7 pp from v18.1-cse
      vs cse-rms's -4.0 pp. Two plausible mechanisms:
        (a) Phase A's MCQ density is low. Phase A is the unified shard
            _kb_mcq_taa_soc_cm_ms_yn -- MCQ rows are diluted by 6 other
            content types, so Phase A at 0.25 likely yields < 10% of
            training tokens being MCQ-shaped, insufficient to hold the
            decoder pattern against the 0.40 catalog-share gradient.
        (b) The standalone TAA shard at 0.35 (absent from cse-rms)
            installs heavy short-form structured generation that
            directly competes with MCQ's short-letter decoder pattern.
            cse-rms had no TAA exposure -> less pattern competition ->
            less MCQ damage. The TAA shard's 0.35 share appears to be
            net negative for MCQ.

      RMS. Hairline (0.28 pp). Cleared §5.1's intent (recovered +8.4 pp
      from v18.1-cse's 46.34) but failed the threshold. v18.2's Phase B
      effective share dropped from cse-rms's 1.0 to 0.40, yielding fewer
      install-shard steps (~600 of 1500 vs cse-rms's full 1250),
      explaining the under-shoot vs cse-rms's 57.6 RMS.

8.2 Diagnosis: the v18.2 -> v18.2.1 rebalance backfired on both target axes
    and dragged two previously-passing axes below their floors.

      8.2.1 MCQ. Phase A 0.25 -> 0.35 was supposed to add MCQ-bearing
            gradient signal sufficient to recover MCQ to >= 70.0. Actual
            delta: +0.84 pp, within run-to-run noise. The 0.10 prob bump
            was nowhere near enough to dent a -9.70 pp regression from
            v18.1-cse. Combined with --max-samples 2400 -> 3000 (+22%
            steps), the larger Stage 4 footprint over-exposed Phase B
            and TAA gradient signal in absolute terms even as Phase A's
            relative share rose -- net effect approximately zero.

      8.2.2 RMS. Phase B 0.40 -> 0.45 was supposed to close the 0.28 pp
            hairline. Actual delta: -4.35 pp -- INVERTED. Mechanism:
            Phase B is a 4-task shard (rms+ate+vsp+rcm); raising its
            interleave probability raises ALL four in lockstep, so the
            RMS-specific signal ratio inside the Stage 4 mix did not
            improve. The +22% step bump then over-trained the dilution.
            Confirmation: ATE -0.80 and RCM -5.75 also regressed under
            the larger Phase B share, consistent with Phase B's multi-
            task structure delivering more catalog-style training
            without preferentially restoring RMS.

      8.2.3 Trade ratio (Stage 4's MCQ-for-RMS exchange):

              variant         dMCQ       dRMS    ratio (|dRMS/dMCQ|)
              -------         ----       ----    -------------------
              cse -> v18.2    -9.70 pp   +8.38   0.86
              cse -> v18.2.1  -8.86 pp   +4.03   0.45

            v18.2.1's trade is half as efficient as v18.2's. Stage 4
            multi-shard at higher prob/step counts loses RMS install
            efficiency without buying back MCQ. The first-order
            implication: the v18.2 prob mix was the right one and
            v18.2 was on the better side of the diminishing-returns
            curve; v18.2.1 pushed past the optimum on both probs and
            step count.

            plausible-accuracy is anomalous and not yet diagnosed --
            track separately when v18.2.2 lands and back-fill v18.2's
