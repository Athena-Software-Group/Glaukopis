AthenaBench-Aligned CTI Templates -- RMS Addendum v6 (companion to ift_data_2026_04_24_v5)

Revision 04252026-RMS-addendum-v6: pure RMS-only addendum intended to be
trained jointly with ift_data_2026_04_24_v5, not as a standalone slate.
The original v6 design (additive on top of v4 RCM/VSP/ATE) was reduced
to RMS-only after the realisation that v5 already supplies adequate
RCM/VSP/ATE coverage; duplicating those sections in v6 would waste
optimizer steps without adding signal. v5 also already contains the
basic AB.RMS.1 (N=2) and AB.RMS.2 (N=1) templates at 500 rows each,
so those are dropped from v6 too.

Combined-training intent (Option B of the v0 -> v6 RMS recovery plan):
  - Training command consumes --dataset ift_data_2026_04_24_v5,
    ift_data_2026_04_25_abaligned_v6, alpaca_en_demo
  - v5 contributes 170,500 rows of broad SFT coverage (including the
    1,000 baseline RMS rows) so the model retains the v5-earned
    capability across TAA, CKT, RCM, VSP, ATE, etc.
  - v6 contributes ~6-8k rows of RMS-specific signal that v5 lacks:
    variable-N lists, catalog flashcards, discriminative negatives.
  - Combined RMS share rises from 0.6% (v5 alone) to ~4-5% (v5+v6).

Why RMS coverage needs this addendum:

  (1) The Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct baseline on athena-rms (500 rows, full
      sweep, 04252026) measures f1=5.88%, plausible_f1=5.97%,
      combined_f1=5.93%. The negligible plausible-vs-strict gap (+0.09pp)
      indicates the model is missing the entire defensive family on most
      rows, not just the exact MITRE-tagged mit. Diagnostic sampling
      shows pronounced anchoring on M1037 with hallucinated descriptions
      ("Restrict System Binaries", "Disable or Modify Tools",
      "Limit Information on Public Websites" -- none of which is what
      M1037 actually is in MITRE's catalog).

  (2) Three concrete distribution gaps between the v5 SFT signal and the
      athena-rms benchmark were identified:

      (a) Prompt distribution: v5's AB.RMS.1/2 use the raw
          {ap.description} (MITRE catalog prose). The benchmark prompt
          is a GPT-5 rewritten incident narrative built per row at
          benchmark generation time. Same underlying technique, very
          different surface form.

      (b) List cardinality: v5's AB.RMS.1 binds exactly two mitigations
          per row (coa1, coa2 with a {force coa1 != coa2} constraint).
          The benchmark prompt explicitly demands "Return exactly N
          mitigation IDs" where N varies from 1 to 8+ across the 500
          rows. The model has no training signal for N!=2 multi-mit
          output.

      (c) ID-to-name binding: v5's AB.RMS.1/2 mention each mitigation's
          name and description once per row in the answer prose, but
          the model is asked to recall ~80 distinct M-IDs across the
          benchmark. The single-row exposure per (technique, mit)
          pair is insufficient repetition for the 8B parameter count
          to lock in the catalog. The hallucinated M1037 descriptions
          observed at evaluation are the direct symptom.

  (3) Train/test split for athena-rms: structurally not feasible without
      crippling SFT coverage. The benchmark samples 500 of ~691
      techniques-with-mitigations from the same MITRE STIX bundle that
      tmpl_gen reads via Neo4j. There is no holdout mechanism in
      create_mitre_attck_data.py and no exclusion list consumed by
      tmpl_gen. Holding out 72% of MITRE's catalog from training to
      eliminate (technique -> mitigation) overlap would defeat the
      purpose of training a CTI model. The literal benchmark prompt
      strings (GPT-5 scenarios) are NOT in the SFT data, so the model
      is not seeing test questions verbatim. We treat athena-rms as
      a catalog-recall benchmark by design and target the distribution
      gaps above, not the underlying graph overlap.

What v6 contains (RMS-only, all genuinely new vs v5):

  - AB.RMS.3a : variable-N multi-mit at N=3, three coa bindings +
                pairwise distinctness. Closes the cardinality gap for
                N=3 benchmark rows. Count: 2000 (Neo4j capped where
                fewer techniques have >=3 mitigations).
  - AB.RMS.3b : variable-N multi-mit at N=4, four coa bindings.
                Count: 2000 (Neo4j capped where fewer techniques have
                >=4 mitigations; expect ~1000-1500 actual rows).
  - AB.RMS.3c : variable-N multi-mit at N=5, five coa bindings.
                Count: 2000 (Neo4j capped; expect ~500-1000 actual).
  - AB.RMS.4  : flashcard, ID -> name + description. Binds against
                course-of-action only (no attack-pattern join), one
                row per mitigation per phrasing. Drills catalog ID
                binding directly to address the M1037-anchoring
                hallucination observed at v5 baseline. Hard catalog
                ceiling at ~44 modern M10xx mitigations -- 3 epochs
                of training gives ~132 catalog exposures per mit.
  - AB.RMS.5  : flashcard, name -> ID. Reverse direction of RMS.4.
                Same single-node binding scope and 44-row ceiling.
  - AB.RMS.6  : free-text discriminative template, "which mitigation
                is NOT recommended for technique X". Replaces the
                negative-example training signal that was lost when
                AB.MCQ.2 was dropped from the v4 slate, without
                re-introducing MCQ format (which caused the v3
                position-A collapse). Single position variant with
                negcoa1 always in slot 1. Count: 2000.

What is dropped vs the original v6 design (now sourced from v5):

  - AB.RCM.1-2   : v5 has 1,000 RCM rows.
  - AB.VSP.1,3   : v5 has 2,000 VSP rows.
  - AB.ATE.1,3,5 : v5 has 2,500 ATE rows.
  - AB.RMS.1     : v5 has 500 N=2 RMS rows. v6's variable-N at N=2
                   is implicitly covered by the v5 baseline.
  - AB.RMS.2     : v5 has 500 N=1 RMS rows.

What remains omitted (same rationale as v4):

  - AB.MCQ.*     : MCQ training coverage held until the v4 -> v5/v6 noMCQ
                   experiment closes the position-A collapse question.
                   (v5 already contains MCQ rows; v6 does not add more.)
  - AB.TAA.*     : TAA training coverage held pending mode-collapse
                   diagnosis and template redesign.

AthenaBench task alignment (v6 RMS-addendum slate):

  Section 4 - AB.RMS.* : attack scenario -> M10xx (list)        -> athena-rms
                         (v5 supplies RCM, VSP, ATE coverage)

Expected dataset effect:

  - Templates: 6 (all RMS, all new vs v5).
  - Row count target: ~6-8k (Neo4j cardinality dependent at N=4, N=5).
  - SFT wall-clock on 2xH100 with v5+v6 combined dataset: ~12-13h
    (matches the v5 reference; the addendum is a small fraction of the
    combined corpus).
  - Combined v5+v6 RMS share: ~4-5% (was 0.6% with v5 alone).
  - Combined v5+v6 RMS template diversity: 8 distinct shapes (was 2).

Template IDs retain the v6 original numbering for traceability against
the dropped RMS.1/RMS.2 (which lived in v5 anyway).

AB.RMS.5 Instruction: You are a cybersecurity architect at an enterprise Security Operations Center consulting the MITRE ATT&CK mitigation catalog. Given the name of a MITRE ATT&CK mitigation, state its identifier and description.

Answer: The mitigation "{coa.name}" has MITRE ATT&CK identifier {coa.mitre_id}: {coa.description}. Therefore, the mitigation identifier is {coa.mitre_id}.
