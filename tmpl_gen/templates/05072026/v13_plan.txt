# v13 Training Plan -- Sophia CTI SFT
Authored 2026-05-07 post-v12 14B benchmark sweep.
Status: planning. Supersedes v12_plan.txt
(tmpl_gen/templates/05052026/v12_plan.txt) for production releases; v12
launchers, HF repos, and dataset entries remain in-repo for
reproducibility and ablation.

## 0. Why v13 exists (v12 post-mortem in one table)

v12 14B shipped at weighted total 57.3, missing the 57.9 §8 bar by
0.6 pp. The build pipeline was correct (all 11 row-count gates green,
260,589 clean rows, 43.3% eval-aligned share) and three of four §8
criteria flipped green. Two regressions and one neutral surface drove
the miss: Phase B's 16384/packing-off recipe dropped RMS, Phase C's
15-step memorisation pass damaged TAA-plausible, and SOC was excluded
from Phase B (forgot the v11 win).

| axis           | v9 14B | v11 14B | v12 14B | v13 target | what v12 told us                                       |
|----------------|-------:|--------:|--------:|-----------:|--------------------------------------------------------|
| weighted total |  56.4  |   54.3  |   57.3  |   >= 58.5  | corpus path was correct; recipe regressed RMS         |
| RMS            |  65.8  |   48.0  |   61.85 |   >= 66.6  | v12 cutoff-16384/packing-OFF bet did not pay off      |
| ATE            |  52.6  |   42.4  |   55.5  |   >= 55.5  | v12 won here; hold                                    |
| VSP            |  86.7  |   70.9  |   85.2  |   >= 85.2  | v10/v12 templates intact; hold                        |
| CKT            |  ~70   |   63.4  |   76.1  |   >= 76.1  | v11 X./YN. trim works; hold                           |
| RCM            |  ~62   |   ~62   |   ~64   |   >= 62.0  | corpus expansion neutral; hold                        |
| SOC            |  ~32   |   44.7  |   35.3  |   >= 44.7  | v12 dropped SOC from Phase B; do NOT repeat           |
| TAA-strict     |  ~16   |   16.0  |   ~22   |   >= 22.0  | Phase C +6 pp strict but -32 pp plausible; net loss   |
| TAA-plausible  |  ~50   |   ~50   |   ~18   |   >= 50.0  | Phase C destroyed plausible attribution; drop Phase C |
| TAA-CANON      |  n/a   |   n/a   |   ~33   |   >= 33.0  | new axis; v12 baseline; hold via Phase A inclusion    |

What v12 did prove (preserve in v13):
- Three-phase training is unnecessary: Phase A+B can carry TAA.CANON
  via main-pass inclusion; Phase C's 15-step memorisation cost more
  than it bought.
- Programmatic generators work: TAA.CANON 10K, CM 6K, MCQ.EXT 3K,
  SOC.GEN 5K all produced clean rows that survived dedup and lifted
  eval-axis density to 43.3%.
- Row-count gate, AB.TAA total cap, stratified shuffle are
  load-bearing; the v12 build pipeline is the v13 substrate.

What v12 failed to prove (no signal -- masked by other variables):
- 16384 cutoff value for RMS catalog: v12 ran cutoff-16384 + packing OFF
  at lr 5e-6; v9 ran cutoff-8192 + packing ON at lr 5e-6; the cutoff
  hypothesis was confounded with the packing change. v13 isolates by
  reverting both knobs to v9 values.
- SOC retention under phased training: v12 excluded SOC from Phase B
  entirely; v13 includes SOC in both phases (matches the v9 shape that
  preserved SOC in v9-derived 14B runs).

      merging the MISP-expanded canon rows into the same Alpaca shard.
  (b) §4.5 NEW licence-allowlist gate (Phase 6b) -- runs after the

      in the merged corpus is outside the permissive allowlist.

### 4.1 Row-count gate updates

`v13_row_count_gate.json` lifts the TAA.CANON floor from 7,500 to
15,000 to detect MISP-merge under-build, lifts the SOC floor from
9,000 back to 10,000 (v11 actuals), and adds an MCQ-combined floor of
10,000 covering AB.MCQ + JS.MCQ + AB.MCQ.EXT + AB.MS.

### 4.2 Stratified shuffle

Unchanged from v12. Phase A's `_broad_plus_canon` shard inherits
stratification; the per-axis interleaving in Phase B's `_axis` shard
carries the v12 §4.2 guarantee.

### 4.3 AB.TAA total-cap

Unchanged from v12. `taa_actor_balance.py --max-rows-per-family-total
3500` enforces the §3.1 ceiling on AB.TAA.{1-5}+JS.TAA.{1-3}.

### 4.4 NEW: MISP TAA generator (Phase 3e)

`tmpl_gen/scripts/misp_taa_generator.py` reads the MISP `threat-actor`
galaxy cluster JSON (CC-0 / public domain; vendored under
`tmpl_gen/data_generation/seeds/misp/threat-actor.json`) and emits
TAA.CANON-shaped Alpaca rows:

  - alias-list -> canonical-name (one row per group with >=2 aliases)
  - canonical-name -> alias-list (mirror direction; reinforces
    bidirectional retrieval)
  - hard-negative pairs (canonical X + alias for canonical Y -> reject)

The generator filters to the union of (a) groups present in MITRE
ATT&CK with cross-reference IDs and (b) groups with multi-element
synonyms in MISP. Output is merged into the same TAA.CANON shard the
v12 MITRE-seed generator already populates; dedup runs after the
merge so cross-source duplicates collapse to a single row.

Expected yield: ~12,000 rows from ~700 distinct canonical groups,
3.7x the v12 MITRE-only ceiling of 187 intrusion-sets.

### 4.5 NEW: licence-allowlist gate (Phase 6b)

`tmpl_gen/scripts/check_corpus_licences.py` walks the merged corpus
and asserts every row's `source` tag is in the permissive allowlist:

  - `mitre-attack-custom`     (MITRE ATT&CK Terms of Use)
  - `misp-galaxy-cc0`         (CC-0 / public domain)
  - `mitre-d3fend-custom`     (MITRE D3FEND Terms of Use)
  - `athena-cti-db-internal`  (this repo's curated knowledge tables)
  - `tulu-3-odc-by`           (AllenAI Tulu-3, ODC-BY 1.0)
  - `alpaca-cc-by-nc-4`       (REJECT for commercial; only in dev builds via --allow-nc)

The build halts before §7 if any row carries a non-allowlisted source
tag (CrowdStrike, Mandiant, ThaiCERT-NC, etc.). Report writes to
`_v13_build/licence_gate_report.json` with the per-row failures.


Split rules:
- Phase B shard (`_axis`): AB.RMS.* + JS.RMS.* + AB.ATE + JS.ATE +
  AB.VSP.* + V.CPE + AB.RCM + JS.RCM + X.VW + YN.VW + SOC.* + SOC.GEN.*
- Phase A shard (`_broad_plus_canon`): everything else (broad knowledge
  + TAA.* + TAA.CANON.* + CM.* + AB.MCQ.* + AB.MCQ.EXT.* + AB.MS.*)

The key change vs v12: TAA.CANON.* moves from a dedicated Phase C
shard into the Phase A shard, and SOC.* moves from Phase A only into
Phase B as well (intersection: SOC sees two epochs of supervision,
which v9's SOC retention shape proved is not over-training).

## 8. Pass criteria for v13 14B production (all six must hold)

- weighted total >= 58.5 (v12 = 57.3; +1.2 pp budgeted from §1 fixes)
- RMS >= 66.6 (v9 65.8 + 0.8 pp; v9 was the recipe v13 reverts to)
- ATE >= 55.5 (v12 hold)
- VSP >= 85.2 (v12 hold)
- CKT >= 76.1 (v12 hold)
- SOC >= 44.7 (v11 floor; v12 regression must be reversed)

Soft criteria (reported, not gating):
- TAA-strict >= 22.0 (v12 floor; MISP expansion should help)
- TAA-plausible >= 50.0 (recover the v12 Phase C damage)
- TAA-CANON >= 33.0 (v12 floor)
- CyberMetric-2000 >= v12; CyberMetric-10000 >= v12
- CyberSOCEval-malware >= v12; CyberSOCEval-TI >= v12

Fallback if Phase B over-corrects RMS at the cost of CKT/CM (v8.1
failure mode): re-introduce a small SOC + CM slice into Phase B (~2K
rows total) to dilute the catalog supervision. SOC is already in
Phase B in v13, so this fallback specifically adds CM rows.

Fallback if MISP expansion over-trains TAA.CANON at the cost of
TAA-attribute: cap MISP yield at 8K rows in Phase A via
`misp_taa_generator.py --max-rows 8000`. The v13 MISP generator emits
in deterministic order so the cap is reproducible.

Fallback if licence-allowlist gate halts the build on legitimate
internal sources: extend the allowlist by adding the source tag to
`tmpl_gen/scripts/check_corpus_licences.py:ALLOWED_SOURCES` and
re-running. Do NOT bypass the gate; the audit trail in
`licence_gate_report.json` is the v13 commercialisation safety net.

## 10. Licensing and commercial-use guardrails (NEW for v13)

v13 is the first vintage authored under an explicit commercial-use
guardrail. The intent is that the v13 14B and 32B checkpoints can be
redistributed (via Hugging Face or downstream packaging) without
inheriting non-commercial restrictions from any training data source.

### 10.1 Allowlist (permissive sources)

| source                  | licence                          | usage in v13                                |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| MITRE ATT&CK            | MITRE ATT&CK Terms of Use        | Groups, Techniques, Mitigations -- core CTI |
| MITRE D3FEND            | MITRE D3FEND Terms of Use        | defensive techniques + counter edges        |
| MISP threat-actor galaxy | CC-0 1.0 / public domain        | TAA.CANON expansion (§4.4)                  |
| MISP mitre-* galaxies   | CC-0 1.0 / public domain         | cross-reference helpers                     |
| AllenAI Tulu-3          | ODC-BY 1.0 (attribution only)    | Phase A CF guard mixture                    |
| athena-cti-db curated tables | this repo's licence         | knowledge tables under tmpl_gen/scripts/    |
| NIST CSF, ISO 27001 (concepts) | factual, not text-redist  | CM.* MCQ stems (paraphrased only)           |

### 10.2 Denylist (NOT used in v13)

| source                  | licence / restriction            | reason for exclusion                         |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| CrowdStrike adversary universe | proprietary               | non-redistributable                          |
| Mandiant APT reports    | proprietary                       | non-redistributable                          |
| ThaiCERT-CSIRT actor cards | CC-BY-NC-SA                   | non-commercial clause incompatible with goal |
| EternalLiberty (StrangerealIntel) | CC-BY-SA-4.0           | share-alike clause -- defer until v14 audit  |
| Recorded Future / Intel471 | proprietary                   | non-redistributable                          |
| Alpaca instruction tuning data | CC-BY-NC-4.0              | only allowed in `--allow-nc` dev builds      |

### 10.3 Audit mechanism

Two complementary build-time gates enforce the policy:

  1. The seed-provenance gate (tmpl_gen/scripts/check_seed_provenance.py,
     run by _v13_build/watcher.sh as Phase 0) verifies that every
     upstream seed file consumed by the build carries an in-tree
     PROVENANCE.txt naming its licence and that the on-disk SHA-256
     matches the value recorded in the script. Registered seeds today:
       - cpt/cache/raw/mitre_attack_enterprise/enterprise-attack.json
         (MITRE ATT&CK Terms of Use)
       - tmpl_gen/data_generation/seeds/misp/threat-actor.json
         (CC-0 1.0 / Public Domain)
       - SFT/test/benchmark_data/athena_bench/athena_taa/aliases.csv
         (Athena internal)

  2. The licence-allowlist gate (tmpl_gen/scripts/check_corpus_licences.py,
     §4.5 in this plan) halts the build before the train/val split if
     any emitted row carries a `source` tag outside the §10.1 allowlist.

Source-tag stamping convention used by the v13 generators:

  - Templates without an explicit `Source:` directive default to
    `athena-cti-db-internal`.
  - The MISP generator emits `misp-galaxy-cc0` (1:1 with the CC-0 seed).
  - The MITRE-seed generators (TAA.CANON, MCQ.EXT, SOC.*.GEN, CM.*)
    emit `athena-cti-db-internal` for their OUTPUT rows because the
    instruction-tuning prompts/answers are Athena-curated transformative
    work (MITRE facts re-expressed as Athena prompts/responses, not
    verbatim text). The upstream MITRE licence chain is recorded in
    cpt/cache/raw/mitre_attack_enterprise/PROVENANCE.txt and verified
    by the Phase 0 seed-provenance gate above.

### 10.4 Open audit deferred to v14

EternalLiberty (CC-BY-SA-4.0) is the largest open-source threat-actor
catalog after MITRE+MISP. Its share-alike clause means including it
would require the v13 checkpoint and dataset to also carry CC-BY-SA;
that's a viable path but requires legal review before the v13 launch.
For v13 it stays out of scope; revisit in v14 if the MISP+MITRE union
under-covers the eval-axis TAA-attribute target.
