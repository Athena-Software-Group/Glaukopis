1. Why v16 exists (one-paragraph overview)
-------------------------------------------

v15 W1 (asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v12-plus-taa) confirmed the v15
parallel-branching topology hypothesis -- the non-target axes were
preserved within bench noise -- but failed to lift TAA Classic above the
v12 baseline. A post-train shard composition audit (taa_actor_balance.py
--dry-run + a shortname-prefix breakdown) revealed the v14 TAA shard
ift_data_2026_05_08_v14_taa was 68.5% Canonical alias-resolution data
(MISP.CANON 35.8% + TAA.CANON 32.7%; combined 22,447 of 32,783 rows).
Canonical alias resolution is NOT measured by the formal TAA Classic
benchmark, so the model spent two-thirds of its training capacity on
a task the score ignores while only ~430 rows (1.3% of the shard)
targeted the JSON-shaped attribution surface that CyberSOCEval-TI
exercises. The v15 plan §3 description ("CANON excluded") was inaccurate
relative to what the v14 watcher actually produced; the watcher's Phase
3 (TAA.CANON generator) and Phase 3e (MISP TAA generator) were the
source of the dilution.

v16 corrects the shard composition by purging Canonical entirely and
bumping the per-template Count: ceiling on every Classic and JSON-shaped
attribution template, plus the IE/NEG hard-negative discrimination
families (full delta table in Sophia-CTI-Templates-v16.txt §"v15 W1 ->
v16 deltas"). The training topology is unchanged. The output checkpoint
is asg-ai/athena-cti-sft-qwen25-14b-v16.

4. Decision criteria (post-bench)
----------------------------------

After the v16 bench result lands, classify the outcome:

  Outcome A -- "v16 ships as the TAA specialist for v15":
    TAA Classic combined > v12 baseline (11.0) + 5pp
    AND every other measured axis >= v12 - 2pp
    AND weighted total >= v12 (57.3)
    -> shard composition was the v15 W1 bottleneck; v16 replaces v15 W1
       as the canonical TAA branch in the parallel architecture
    -> proceed to v15 W2 (additional specialists / SOC corpus authoring /
       mergekit integration) per v15_plan.txt §5

  Outcome B -- "v16 moves TAA but regresses an adjacent axis":
    TAA Classic up but one or more axes regressed > 2pp vs v12
    -> escalate to merge sweep with v12 (alpha sweep over Linear or
       Task Arithmetic; see v15_plan.txt §4 Outcome B)

  Outcome C -- "v16 still doesn't move TAA":
    TAA Classic combined <= v12 baseline + 5pp
    -> the bottleneck is template-bound TAA accuracy ceiling (5%-accurate
       vs 93%-plausible pattern observed in v14.1 D-TAA), not shard
       composition; v16 falsifies the dilution hypothesis
    -> halt v16; author a v17 manifest with explicit hard-negative
       attribution pairs (correct + 2-3 close-but-wrong) and chain-of-
       thought rationales, AND/OR explore recipe adjustments (lr bump
       to 1e-5, 2-epoch run with cosine schedule)

  Outcome D -- "net regression regardless of TAA":
    Weighted total < v12 (57.3)
    -> diagnose before any deployment; do not ship
